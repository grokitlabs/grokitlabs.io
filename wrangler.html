Why teams switch

Simpler than a suite. More honest than five of them.

Every agency has the same problem: the truth lives in five tools, and four of them are out of date. Each suite promises to govern itself — with retention policies, versioning settings, and admin consoles you'll never touch. Wrangler takes the opposite approach: one simple place, tended by an agent, that works across everything you already use.

Change everything, review once

Tell Wrangler what changed — a price, a policy, a process. It updates every affected document and hands you one review, like a bank statement. Approve it or roll it all back.

One truth across every tool

Word, Google Docs, Notion, Excel — Wrangler audits across all of them, sniffing out contradictions and stale references no single suite can see, and flags them before a client does. Then it can produce what you need next — the proposal, the deck, the one-pager — from the same source of truth.

Leave anytime — it's your stuff

Everything in Wrangler is a plain file you own. Take the whole knowledge base to your laptop, your phone, or a competitor — no export hostage negotiations, ever. Word docs and Google links welcome too.

How it works

Gradual by design

No big-bang migration. Wrangler meets your documents where they are — and quietly moves your business onto ground you own.

1 · Start as you are

Connect your files exactly as they exist — Word, Excel, Notion, Drive. Wrangler audits and syncs them in place. Nothing to convert, nothing to learn.

2 · The truth consolidates

The facts every document shares — pricing, policies, boilerplate, process — settle into plain text files: the one format readable by any tool, any AI, any decade, with no vendor and no subscription required. That's not a technical detail. That's your business's knowledge, finally on ground you own.

3 · Everything derives

Proposals, decks, SOPs, and one-pagers are produced from that source of truth, in the formats your clients expect. Change the truth once — everything downstream follows, with your approval.

Where this goes

Full project management. Same source of truth.

Wrangler includes real project and workflow management — projects, tasks, boards, pipelines, owners, deadlines, custom workflows — flexible enough to replace Trello or Jira for most teams. Not a status widget bolted onto documents: a first-class system for running the work itself.

The difference from every other tracker: your projects and your documents share one source of truth. The proposal a task refers to is right there, linked and current — never a copy, never a stale attachment. Anyone in the company can see where anything stands and open the document behind it. No second tool, no per-seat workflow tax. And like a good database, Wrangler enforces one copy of every fact — when truth tries to duplicate or drift, the agent catches it at the door.

Why "Wrangler"

I know a thing or two about consistency. I've driven the same 1999 Jeep Wrangler TJ since 2003. When I got my golden retriever, I needed a name that was a tribute to a dog named CJ, and thus TJ was born. So when I built a tool whose whole job is rounding up scattered, drifting documents and keeping them faithful and true — well, the name and mascot was waiting for me.

I built Wrangler because I've lived the pain of the truth living in five places and AI advancements makes solving this problem much easier. Every business I've run or worked with kept a wiki that was wrong, proposals that quoted last year's prices, and SOPs nobody trusted. The fix was never "try harder" — it was making documents that maintain themselves, with a human holding the reins.

That's Wrangler. TJ supervises.
