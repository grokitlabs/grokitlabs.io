No code · no apps to install · ~30 minutes to set up

Run your projects with Claude

One folder. Your email. And Claude doing the filing, the status updates, and the "what needs me today" — so you never maintain anything. Everything lives in plain files, in your own Drive, that you keep forever.

The three players

Every diagram below is just these three passing work between them.

You
Make decisions. Press send. That's the whole job.
Claude
Reads, files, updates, drafts, and reminds. The assistant.
Your stuff
Your email + one Drive folder. Yours, always.

Part 1 — The setup (once)

Three steps, one conversation.

you

Connect your email & Drive to Claude

In Claude's settings, connect Gmail and Google Drive. This lets Claude read your mail and read/write one folder — nothing else.

then
you

Create one folder: "My Projects"

One Drive folder is the whole system. Claude organizes inside it; you can open any file anytime — they're all plain, readable documents.

📁 My Projects
📄 how-we-file-things.md ← the filing rules you two agree on
📁 Projects ← one page per project, with its status
📄 kitchen-renovation.md · 📄 hendricks-proposal.md
📁 Documents ← proposals, contracts, notes — filed & linked
📄 today.md ← your daily summary lands here
then
you + claude · together

Agree on the filing system — once, out loud

Tell Claude how you think about your work; Claude proposes how to organize it and writes the agreement into how-we-file-things.md. From then on Claude follows those rules — and asks before changing them.

"I run projects for clients, plus house stuff. Every project should have a status, its related documents linked on its page, and nothing should ever be filed loose."
finally
you

Ask Claude to check in twice a day

Set up two recurring check-ins in Claude: a morning summary and an evening tidy-up. You describe them in plain English once; they run on their own after that — even when your computer is off.

"Every morning at 7, read my new email and my Projects folder, update anything that changed, and write me a short summary with what needs me today."

Part 3 — The three rules that make it trustworthy

Claude proposes. You approve.Drafts, not sent emails. Suggested changes, not silent ones. Nothing leaves your world without your finger on the button.
Everything is a plain file you can open.No app, no lock-in. Stop using Claude tomorrow and the folder still works, forever, with anything.
The filing rules are written down — by both of you.The system is only as tidy as its rules. They live in one file you agreed on, and Claude asks before changing them.

Grow it later, not now: once the daily loop feels boring (that's success), the same three players handle more — a weekly "what's stuck?" review, watching for late invoices, prepping you before meetings. Same folder, same rules, more check-ins.
