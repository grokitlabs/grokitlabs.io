A how-to, not a product · no code · ~30 minutes

How I run my projects with Claude

This is the exact setup I use: one project folder, my email, and Claude doing the filing, the status updates, and the "what needs me today." I never maintain it. Everything lives in plain files, in my own Drive, that I keep forever — and you can copy this in about half an hour.

A few friends asked how I set this up. There's no app to download and nothing to sign up for beyond Claude itself — just a way of using it.

The three things doing the work

Every step below is just these three passing work between them — nothing else involved.

Me
Make decisions. Press send. That's the whole job.
Claude
Reads, files, updates, drafts, and reminds.
My stuff
My email + one Drive folder. Mine, always.

Part 1 — The setup (once)

Three steps, one conversation with Claude. Here's exactly what I did.

me

Connect email & Drive to Claude

In Claude's settings, connect Gmail and Google Drive. This lets Claude read your mail and work in your Drive. One honest heads-up: connecting Drive gives Claude access to your whole Drive, not just one folder — that's how Google's connection works today. So if you keep private things in Drive, the clean fix is to make a separate Google account just for this and connect that one. Then "your whole Drive" is a Drive that holds only your projects. (This is the one fiddly step — worth doing once. It's what I did.)

then
me

Make one folder — I called mine "Projects"

One Drive folder is the whole system. Claude organizes inside it; you can open any file anytime — they're all plain, readable documents. Here's roughly what mine looks like:

📁 Projects
📄 how-we-file-things.md ← the filing rules Claude and I agreed on
📁 Projects ← one page per project, with its status
📄 kitchen-renovation.md · 📄 hendricks-proposal.md
📁 Documents ← proposals, contracts, notes — filed & linked
📄 today.md ← my daily summary lands here
then
me + claude

Agree on the filing system — once, out loud

Tell Claude how you think about your work; Claude proposes how to organize it and writes the agreement into how-we-file-things.md. From then on Claude follows those rules — and asks before changing them. This is what I told it:

"I run projects for clients, plus house stuff. Every project should have a status, its related documents linked on its page, and nothing should ever be filed loose."
finally
me

Ask Claude to check in twice a day

Set up two recurring check-ins in Claude: a morning summary and an evening tidy-up. Describe them in plain English once; they run on their own after that — even when your computer is off. Here's what I asked for:

"Every morning at 7, read my new email and my Projects folder, update anything that changed, and write me a short summary with what needs me today."

Part 2 — Then this happens every day

A real example from my own folder — the proposal email, start to finish, and I never touched it.

my email

A contractor sends a proposal for my kitchen renovation. It needs my review. I'm busy — I don't even open it.

morning check-in
claude

Claude reads it and does the secretary work

Following the filing rules we agreed on, Claude: saves the proposal into Documents → links it on the kitchen-renovation project page → changes the project's status to "Proposal — needs your review."

my move
me

I decide — in one sentence

"Looks high. Draft a reply asking for a cost breakdown and whether the timeline includes permits."

Claude writes the reply into my drafts — I read it and press send. Claude never sends anything itself.

evening tidy-up
claude

Everything gets updated — everywhere

Project status → "Waiting on contractor." The question I asked → noted on the project page. Tomorrow's summary will chase it if he goes quiet. The folder is current, and I never touched it.

Part 3 — The three rules that make it trustworthy

Claude proposes. You approve.Drafts, not sent emails. Suggested changes, not silent ones. Nothing leaves your world without your finger on the button.
Everything is a plain file you can open.No app, no lock-in. Stop using Claude tomorrow and the folder still works, forever, with anything.
The filing rules are written down — by both of you.The system is only as tidy as its rules. They live in one file you agreed on, and Claude asks before changing them.
Where I'd take it next, once the daily loop feels boring (that's success): the same three players can handle more — a weekly "what's stuck?" review, watching for late invoices, prepping before meetings. Same folder, same rules, just more check-ins. That's the whole setup. If you copy it and find a better way to do a piece of it, I'd genuinely like to hear about it.

One honest note: this isn't Claude-exclusive. ChatGPT and other assistants have similar Gmail/Drive connectors and scheduled tasks now, so this same pattern works there too. I use Claude — but the idea is the pattern, not the brand.
